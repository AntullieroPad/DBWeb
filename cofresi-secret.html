El Pirata Legendario

Roberto Cofresí y Ramírez de Arellano (1791-1825), better known as "El Pirata Cofresí," was Puerto Rico's most famous pirate. Born in Cabo Rojo, he became a legendary figure whose exploits have been woven into Puerto Rican folklore.

While Spanish authorities and foreign merchants considered him a dangerous criminal, many Puerto Ricans viewed him as a Robin Hood figure. Legends claim he shared his bounty with the poor and protected local fishermen from harassment by colonial authorities.

Cofresí commanded a swift schooner named El Mosquito (The Mosquito) and was known for his daring raids against Spanish ships and other merchant vessels in the Caribbean. His crew was fiercely loyal, and his knowledge of the coastal waters around Puerto Rico allowed him to evade capture for years.

His rebellion against colonial powers came during a time of great economic hardship in Puerto Rico, and many locals saw his activities as acts of resistance against Spanish rule. Eventually, a joint operation between Spanish and American naval forces captured Cofresí in March 1825. He was executed by firing squad in El Morro Castle in San Juan.
