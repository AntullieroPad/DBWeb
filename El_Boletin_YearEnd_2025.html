This year was different. Not because of the events but because of what it felt like. There was a moment, not too long ago, when DB was barely holding on. COVID hit us hard. But I think that's what happens when you find community.
You love very unreasonably.

This year, we brought music. We brought dance. We brought food (40 pounds of it, seasoned and slow-roasted). We saw legends brought to campus: a Surgeon General, an artist who painted while he spoke, economists debating our island's future. We dug through archives and found ourselves in the pages. We marched through Hartford with flags and drums in our hands.

And every Friday, we gathered for cafecito. Sometimes two people, five people, and sometimes even twenty. While the numbers changed, what mattered was that we showed up.
