II. Curated Reading Lists

A. Foundational Texts & Scholarly Works

  • Historical and Political Context: Consider reading José Trías Monge’s Puerto Rico: The Trials of the Oldest Colony in the World and selections from Arlene Dávila (e.g. Barrio Dreams or Latino Spin).
  • Contemporary Perspectives: Explore essays on statehood, diaspora identity, and neoliberal impacts on Puerto Rican communities.

B. Online Reading Collections

  • Library of Congress – Puerto Rico: Hispanic Reading Room Research Guide
  • Freely Available Resources on Puerto Rican History & Culture (Brown LibGuides)

III. Digital Archives & Online Collections

A. Academic & Archival Resources

  • CentroPR – Center for Puerto Rican Studies Library & Archives
  • Biblioteca Digital Puertorriqueña / Puerto Rican Digital Library
  • Chronicling America – Puerto Rican Newspapers

B. Specialized Digital Projects

  • Ciencia Puerto Rico - A nonprofit portal supporting STEM education.
  • Puerto Rican Studies Subject Guides (UConn, UCLA)

IV. Multimedia & Podcasts

A. Audio & Video Resources

  • La Brega: Stories of the Puerto Rican Experience - A bilingual podcast exploring Puerto Rican resilience.
  • Yale Archives Video (2021) - A bilingual (English/Spanish) video recording from Yale’s Manuscripts and Archives, potentially addressing contemporary Puerto Rican issues or activism 13.
  • Puerto Rico's Debt Crisis Explained by CNBC - CNBC explains the background and complexities of Puerto Rico's debt crisis, a major factor in its contemporary challenges.
  • Look for recorded discussions hosted by Yale, UConn, and other universities.

VI. Guiding Questions for Deeper Study

  • Identity & Diaspora: How do colonial legacies and diaspora experiences shape contemporary Puerto Rican identity?
  • Cultural Resilience: How do literature, music, and oral histories reflect the “brega” (struggle)?
  • Political and Social Transformation: How have debates over political status influenced cultural and social policies?
  • Digital Humanities: What is the impact of digital archives on preserving Puerto Rican history?
  • Science & Culture: How do initiatives like Ciencia Puerto Rico contribute to progress?
